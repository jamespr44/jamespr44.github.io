About

Who I Am & Why Engineering

I came to engineering the way most engineers do — as a kid who couldn't leave things alone. Taking things apart, reading about how technology worked, and asking why things were designed the way they were. That curiosity led me to study Mechanical Engineering at UTS, and it's still the thing that drives me every day.

My goal has always been clear: work on projects that have a tangible, positive impact on people. Not abstract impact — real, physical infrastructure that people use every day and that improves the way a city functions.

Since 2023 I've been with Fredon as an Undergraduate Engineer and Project Manager, gaining hands-on experience across some of Sydney's most complex and high-profile building services projects. From commissioning HVAC and life safety systems at an internationally award-winning metro station, to contributing to the design and delivery of a six-star hotel at Circular Quay, to helping design and manage Australia's premier biomedical laboratory — I've been fortunate to work on projects that genuinely matter.

These experiences have shaped my understanding of what engineering looks like in practice: it's technically demanding, commercially driven, and deeply dependent on the ability to communicate clearly across disciplines and stakeholder groups. That's the kind of work I want to keep doing.

Internship Reflection

What I Learned at Fredon

A reflection on two years of hands-on experience in building services contracting — the expectations, the surprises, and the lessons that will shape my career.

Most Important Lessons

The two most important lessons were stakeholder communication and subcontractor management. On communication, I quickly learned that clearly conveying technical information to people with varying levels of engineering knowledge — clients, project managers, and tradespeople on-site — was both the hardest and most critical skill to develop. Misunderstandings at this level cost time and money. On subcontractor management, keeping multiple parties accountable requires firmness and relationship-building in equal measure. These skills cannot be taught in a classroom.

Career Direction

My internship has firmly pointed me toward a career in project management within building services or construction. Seeing how effective project management directly determines whether a project is delivered on time and within budget — and experiencing the satisfaction of driving that outcome myself — confirmed that this is the kind of work I want to pursue. The breadth of technical and commercial exposure I have gained places me in a strong position relative to most graduating students.

Expectations Before Starting

Before beginning at Fredon, I had limited insight into what working in building services contracting would look like in practice. I assumed it would differ significantly from the theoretical environment of university — particularly given that commercial engineering is largely financially driven. I anticipated needing to develop financial literacy quickly and expected a reasonable amount of time on-site. Beyond that, much of the day-to-day reality was unknown to me.

The Reality

The experience largely aligned with my expectations, though in some ways it surprised me. The engineering work was more high-level than I anticipated — rather than hands-on calculations from first principles, the focus was on system implementation, design compliance, and working efficiently within established frameworks and teams. What genuinely surprised me was the scale of the commercial and procurement dimension: exposure to contract negotiations, budget management, and subcontractor coordination was something I had not previously considered.

Value Proposition to an Employer

My value proposition is the combination of technical engineering knowledge and hands-on project management experience at a level uncommon for a graduating student. Having independently run a fit-out project, contributed to major infrastructure at Sydney Metro Gadigal Station, helped design and manage the Sydney Biomedical Accelerator and the Waldorf Astoria mechanical package, and managed subcontractors and procurement across multiple live projects, I can demonstrate the ability to add real value from day one. My track record of operating with minimal supervision and receiving commendation for my communication are concrete examples I can speak to in any professional setting.
